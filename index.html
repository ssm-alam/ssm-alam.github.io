Research Interest

Cancer immunotherapy has emerged as a powerful strategy for controlling cancer progression by harnessing the host immune system. Immune checkpoint molecules such as PD-1/PD-L1, CTLA-4, and VISTA play critical roles in tumor-mediated immune suppression. My research interest lies in understanding the structural and functional mechanisms governing these immune checkpoints and exploring computational strategies to modulate their activity. By targeting immune checkpoint signaling, this work aims to contribute to the development of novel therapeutic strategies for effective cancer immunotherapy.
