Research Experience

My research focuses on structure-guided cancer immunotherapy with a primary emphasis on immune checkpoint molecules such as PD-1/PD-L1, CTLA-4, and VISTA. I apply computational approaches including molecular docking, molecular dynamics simulations, and immunoinformatics to investigate immune checkpoint regulation and therapeutic modulation.

My doctoral work integrates protein structure analysis, epitope prediction, mutation impact assessment, and simulation-based validation to support the rational design of immune checkpoint inhibitors and vaccine candidates.

Projects

PD-L1 Multi-epitope Cancer Vaccine

Designed a multi-epitope cancer vaccine targeting PD-L1 using immunoinformatics. CTL, HTL, and B-cell epitopes were predicted, assembled, and validated through molecular docking, molecular dynamics simulations, and immune response modeling.

Structural Impact of nsSNPs in CD274

Investigated the effects of non-synonymous SNPs on PD-L1 structure, stability, and interaction with PD-1 using structure prediction, stability analysis, and simulation-based approaches.
